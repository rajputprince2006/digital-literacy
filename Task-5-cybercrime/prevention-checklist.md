
## Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account, combining letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like birthdays or names.
2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on email, social media, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security.
3. **Be cautious with emails, links, and attachments**—do not click on unknown or suspicious messages. Verify the sender before taking action.
4. **Regularly update software and apps** to patch vulnerabilities that cybercriminals could exploit.
5. **Protect UPI and online banking information**: never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank passwords with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank.
6. **Verify payment requests** before transferring money via UPI or apps like Google Pay, Paytm, or PhonePe. Confirm the recipient personally if possible.
7. **Secure your devices**: use antivirus software, keep your phone and laptop locked with PINs, and avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions.
8. **Limit sharing personal information online**—avoid posting sensitive details like address, phone number, or college ID publicly on social media.
9. **Backup important data** regularly to cloud storage or external drives to prevent loss from ransomware or device theft.
10. **Report cybercrime incidents promptly**: use the **National Cyber Crime Portal ([cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in))** or call the **Helpline 1930** for guidance.
